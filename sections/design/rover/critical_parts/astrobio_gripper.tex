The gripper is designed to collect a defined volume of liquid from a cup on the
ground and to measure its pH. Its main objective is to sample up to [TBD] mL while
remaining within the defined capacity limit of [TBD] mL. The subsystem combines a gripper-side 
controller, a non-contact liquid-level sensor, and an analog pH measurement board. 
A peristaltic pump operates until the sensor confirms that the required volume has been reached. 
Once the target level is detected, the pump stops and the pH measurement is transmitted to the 
rover main computer and onward to the ground station. The schematic and operating concept 
are shown in \autoref{fig:astrobio_gripper_scheme} and \autoref{fig:astrobio_gripper_operation}.
