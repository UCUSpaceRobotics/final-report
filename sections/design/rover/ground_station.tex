The ground station is the fixed operator post for driving the rover,
commanding its subsystems, and monitoring autonomous runs. It links to the
tripod-mounted directional antenna over an Ethernet (PoE) run of up to
\qty{20}{\meter} and packs into a ruggedised flip-lid case
(\autoref{fig:gs_sizes_scheme}) deployable in \qty{30}{\minute}.

Two operators crew the station (\autoref{fig:gs_operators}): the primary
drives and commands the manipulator, the second monitors telemetry and holds
the emergency stop; both are cross-trained and swap by task. A workstation
decodes the video streams, hosts the browser-based operator UI, and runs the
\texttt{rosbridge} server into the ROS~2 graph, with a backup laptop able to
take over within \qty{30}{\minute}. Two monitors carry the camera feeds and
the telemetry and system-health view, while manual input comes from a custom
console of three joysticks plus buttons and switches for driving, subsystem
toggles, and mode selection. A latching emergency-stop button is wired to an
independent \qty{433}{\mega\hertz} LoRa transceiver that bypasses Wi-Fi,
ROS~2, and the station PC, surviving their failure.
