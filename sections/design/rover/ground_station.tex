The ground station is the fixed operator post from which the rover is
teleoperated. It is deployed inside the team zone and stays within a
\qty{20}{\meter} radius of the directional antenna it feeds. The station is
crewed by two operators (\autoref{fig:gs_operators}) working
interchangeably: either operator can drive the rover or command the
manipulator, and the two roles are reassigned between them as each task
demands.

Its core equipment comprises:
\begin{itemize}
    \item \textbf{Ground station PC} --- a desktop workstation that drives
    both monitors, decodes the incoming video streams, and displays
    telemetry; it hosts the operator UI and the \texttt{rosbridge} client
    that carries commands to the rover.
    \item \textbf{Displays} --- two monitors, one dedicated to live camera
    feeds and one to the telemetry and mission dashboard.
    \item \textbf{Manual controls} --- a custom operator console that
    combines three joysticks with physical buttons and switches for driving,
    subsystem toggles, and control-mode selection.
    \item \textbf{Network equipment} --- Mast Computer links the
    workstation with the tripod-mounted directional antenna over an Ethernet
    (PoE) run; the antenna carries the primary \qty{5}{\giga\hertz} Wi-Fi
    link, detailed in the following section.
\end{itemize}
