The rover and Ground Station (GS) communicate over three independent radio links: primary, fallback,
and E-Stop.
 
The \textbf{primary link} is 5\,GHz 802.11ac Wi-Fi (Alfa AWUS036ACH on the rover, hostapd AP on a
mast-mounted mast computer with Alfa AWUS036ACH ), bridged over Ethernet + PoE to the GS Mini~PC. The rover runs ROS~2, and
the GS Mini~PC has ROS~2 nodes on the same network, communicating directly with the rover's nodes --
no bridging software layer is used.
 
If the primary link is lost, the system automatically switches, after a timeout to the 
\textbf{fallback link}: 430--440\,MHz LoRa (Ebyte E32-433T30D), carrying a
lightweight binary frame with essential motion commands 
 
The \textbf{E-Stop link} is a separate, dedicated 433.92 \,MHz channel that carries only the
emergency-stop signal triggered by a physical E-Stop button on the GS. It is made the simpliest possible 
to minimize the risk of failure, and is not used for any other purpose. 
The E-Stop link is always active, even if the primary and fallback links are down.
