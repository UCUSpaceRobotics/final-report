The unified sample gripper replaces the standard gripper fingers with opposing bucket scoops while retaining the dual-linkage mechanism. The buckets provide a total volume of \qty{700}{\cubic\centi\meter} and collect at least \qty{200}{\gram} of regolith per pass. Rubber-lined rims and full wrist articulation enable secure handling of rocks up to \qty{15}{\centi\meter}. A perpendicular Archimedean auger, shoulder-driven and mounted as an end-effector component, has a length of \qty{335}{\milli\meter} and a diameter of \qty{35}{\milli\meter}. The auger tip is widened to prevent tube separation, and the internal screw is machined to be \qty{4}{\milli\meter} smaller in diameter than the tube inner radius, ensuring friction-free rotation. The auger length was selected to capture and hold approximately \qty{100}{\gram} of drilled soil, while enabling penetration beyond \qty{30}{\centi\meter} with minimal distal mass. The complete system is illustrated in \autoref{fig:unified_sample_gripper_scheme} and \autoref{fig:unified_sample_gripper_operation}.
